29!

Introduction:

28 is usually played by four players in fixed partnerships, partners facing each other. 32 cards from a standard 52-card pack are used for play. There are eight cards in each of the usual "French" suits: hearts, diamonds, clubs and spades. The cards in every suit rank from high to low: J-9-A-10-K-Q-8-7. The aim of the game is to win tricks containing valuable cards.

Rules:

  • The game is played between 4 players wherein 2 people partner up together. 32 cards from the standard 52 card pack are used for the game.
  • The 2, 3, 4 and 5 cards from each suite are removed from the deck. Each player gets one set. These are used as trump cards.
  • Jacks, Nines, Aces and Tens carry 3,2,1 and 1 points respectively. Each player has four of these. This gives everyone 28 points in hand.
  • Kings and Queens carry no points.
  • The goal is to play tricks where you have the highest valued cards. The final trick has 1 extra point bringing the total to 29.

Other card games:

Bridge

Contract bridge, or simply bridge, is a trick-taking card game using a standard 52-card deck. In its basic format, it is played by four players in two competing partnerships, with partners sitting opposite each other around a table. Millions of people play bridge worldwide in clubs, tournaments, online and with friends at home, making it one of the world's most popular card games, particularly among seniors.

Bluff

Bluff is a fun card game where players try to lie without getting caught so they can get rid of all their cards first. To play, first deal all of the cards in a standard deck out to the players one at a time.

Poker

Poker is a family of comparing card games in which players wager over which hand is best according to that specific game's rules in ways similar to these rankings. While the earliest known form of the game was played with just 20 cards, today it is usually played with a standard deck, although in countries where short packs are common, it may be played with 32, 40 or 48 cards.
